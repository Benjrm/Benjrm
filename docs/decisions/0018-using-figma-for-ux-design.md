# Using Figma for UX Design

## Context and Problem Statement

The team needs a tool for creating wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes to plan and communicate the UI before implementation.
The challenge is to find a tool that supports real-time collaboration, is accessible to all team members without a paid license, and covers the full design workflow from rough wireframe to clickable prototype.

## Considered Options

* Figma
* Adobe XD
* Balsamiq

## Decision Outcome

Chosen option: "Figma", because it covers the entire design workflow in a single tool and is accessible to all team members through its free tier.

Figma supports wireframing, high-fidelity mockups, and interactive prototypes in one place, eliminating the need to switch between tools at different design stages. Its browser-based approach means no installation is required and all team members can collaborate in real time on the same file.

Compared to Adobe XD, Figma requires no Adobe subscription and has a more active community with a larger library of free plugins and UI kits. Adobe XD has also been discontinued by Adobe, making it a poor long-term choice.

Compared to Balsamiq, Figma supports both low-fidelity wireframes and high-fidelity mockups, whereas Balsamiq is limited to rough sketches and cannot produce polished prototypes for user testing.
